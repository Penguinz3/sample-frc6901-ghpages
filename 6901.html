Knights Robotics is a non-profit FIRST Robotics Competition team in Frisco, TX — founded in 2018 to inspire the next generation of STEM leaders.

About Us

Built for
Students

Knights Robotics is a non-profit FIRST Robotics Competition team that nurtures high school talent through STEM-based learning. We encourage the practical application of STEM in our community through robotics. Students develop technical skills including CAD, programming, and machining, alongside teamwork, problem-solving, and leadership through subteams and community events. Founded in 2018, we have competed across Texas and qualified for the FRC World Championship during the Deep Space season.

"More than robots — we build engineers, communicators, and collaborators."
About FRC

The
Competition

"The World's Leading Youth-Serving Nonprofit Advancing STEM Education"

Combining the excitement of sport with the rigors of science and technology, FIRST Robotics Competition is the ultimate Sport for the Mind. High-school participants call it "the hardest fun you'll ever have." Under strict rules and limited time, teams design, fund, and build industrial-size robots to play a difficult field game. Volunteer professional mentors guide each team toward a season-ending Championship. More at firstinspires.org →

Our History

Season by
Season

2023
Charged Up
A remarkable season — 3rd place finish, 10-5-0 record

Secured 12th place in the first event, then achieved a 3rd place finish and earned the Team Sustainability Award. Unexpectedly qualified for the state tournament — a proud milestone. Though state was tough, the team's dedication shone all season.

2022
Rapid React
District Event Finalist — competed with top Texas powerhouses

Two district events — Fort Worth and Irving. At Irving, ranked 33rd but climbed through the bracket to reach the finals alongside Team 118 Robonauts, Team 148 Robowranglers, and Team 3310 Black Hawk Robotics. Won the District Event Finalist award.

2021
Virtual Challenge
Engineering Design Award — FIRST Game Design Challenge

Despite the pandemic, participated in the FIRST Game Design Challenge and FIRST Innovation Challenge (sponsored by Qualcomm). Earned the Engineering Design Award for a methodical approach to building the game field design.

2020
Infinite Recharge
Season cut short — pivoted to STEM community education

Season cut short by the pandemic after one conference. The team channeled their energy into online educational programs on the fundamentals of robotics for students and college attendees at partner institutions.

2019
Deep Space
FRC World Championship qualifiers — Houston, TX

Placed 8th at Plano (Innovation in Control Award) and 5th at Dallas (Quality Award). Competed at FiT District Championships in Austin, reached the quarterfinals, and qualified for the FIRST Championship in Houston — a historic achievement.

2018
Power Up
Founding season — 34th of 60 at Dallas Regionals

As a first-year team, finished 34th out of 60 at the Dallas Regionals. A foundational year — the competition experience and talent of other teams inspired the team to build toward greater heights in every season that followed.

Community

Outreach

Team 6901 is dedicated to providing STEM educational opportunities for students of all ages — from elementary to college level — with a focus on practical robotics.

University of Brasilia — LAMFO

Virtually assisted a college in Brazil through instructional videos on robotic mechanisms and designs, plus an introductory programming lesson — sharing expertise with a global audience.

FIRST Lego League Camp

Hosted a camp for elementary and middle schoolers introducing the engineering design process, block coding, and foundational sensor usage — sparking early STEM interest.

AOPS Virtual Camp

Assisted middle school students with robotics programming in a virtual simulator and engaged them in teamwork through assigned missions — expanding the team's educational reach online.
